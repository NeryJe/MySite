Project Summary

iFocus is created to help students achieve their goals successfully. I spent time finding a solution to make a student's journey less stressful. iFocus is a tool to help students to stay focused and have an organized method to keep track of their time spent, as well as having a community where they can support each other by sharing their journey, ideas, etc..

The Challenge

While students try their best to have a successful career, it can be difficult trying to find solutions that can help them to achieve their goals. Students can encounter a vast number of challenges during their study period. Staying focused and organized is one of the primary issues when accomplishing a task. But yes, there are many tools that can help with academic performance. Not having the right tools can be detrimental for a student’s career. It can be very difficult getting assignments done while having a social life. Having too many distractions can affect a student’s academics. While not only students face these issues, anyone can face this issue trying to complete a personal task or a study assignment.

Solution

Users have a vast amount of information about the best practices to stay focused. Since this can feel overwhelming for users, in iFocus my goal is to help students achieve their goals in a clear and efficient way. Simply by adding their task and keeping track of the time spent their goals can be achieved. Selecting their own time to focus and time to rest this will help them keep control over their time spent. Also, providing community support where all the students can interact and share their journey.

Discovery

Secondary Research

Conducting this research I concluded that students have a long range of challenges while studying. These problems may vary in the student's personal life and external factors.  By not recognizing this challenge can be damaging for the student. Some students may not be aware of these issues so they tend to  keep making the same mistakes during their study period. 

The primary points of my research will be common issues such as; having a lack of time management, and too many distractions.

Primary Research

To get insight as to how users can stay focused and productive during their study period, I used screener surveys to recruit participants. After interviewing them and collecting more data I was able to synthesize my findings into affinity groups, empathy maps, and personas.

The main takeaway from these findings are; evaluations of students behaviors during their study hours. Understanding their needs, motivations, and their mental health.

Survey

When creating the screen survey the main objective was to find participants who’ve had a hard time staying focused or organizing their time as well those who have been successful.

Participants characteristics:

  • Don't have a plan for their study routine.
  • They tend to feel bored, distracted and/or anxious.
  • They are former students.

Interviews

I conducted these interviews with five participants to understand students’ behavior and their personal lifestyle. The goal of these interviews was to analyze and understand the “why” of their problems and how they come to face challenges when studying.

Affinity Mapping

With my interviews I was able to narrow it down to six categories: these included problems when studying, methods used, feelings, areas to improve and expectations, favorite activities, and motivations.

Ideation

Empathy map

Affinity maps were then categorized into two empathy maps, the unorganized student and the organized student.

Persona 1

The unorganized student: She works as a part time barista, after work she attends classes online. She Sometimes studies at home, in the cafeteria, or at the library. She tries to improve her study habits everyday. In her free time she likes to stream movies, read a book, and go out with friends. She likes to be active and likes to spend time on her hobbies like crochet.

Persona 2

The organized student: She works as a waitress on the weekends. When she’s not working she goes to school in the mornings. After school, when she gets home she checks if there's any assignments that need to be completed. To keep track of time she tends to use the pomodoro technique to track time. After studying, she likes to take breaks and go to the park, or gym. In her free time she likes to spend time with her friends or stream movies.

HMW’s 

The final piece of my research was to analyze the data, with How Might We (HMW) statements to clearly communicate the most important problems users want solved. 

How might we:

  • Create a system that will help students to stay focused during their study time?
  • Help students to feel more motivated when they start studying a subject?
  • Create a solution that will make students feel motivated to keep studying?
  • Make students feel supported during their study period?

Ideate

After focusing on HMW’s it was finally time to ideate and begin sketching screens to find possible solutions to solve the users problems.

User Stories

Before I could get started with designing I had to identify the most important functions this app needs.

User stories were categorized by priority, the highest being the most crucial functions, this helped me understand the necessary features and elements that needed to be implemented for a smooth experience.

As a student, I want to;

High Priority

  • I want to track my study time for each subject so that I can have a clear view of my time spent.
  • I want to have some kind of motivational quotes so that I can feel better when studying.
  • I want to have a community with students just like me so that I can feel less lonely.

Medium Priority

  • I want to be able to block my phone from distractions while using the study app so that I can focus better.
  • I want to be able to see my progress on all subjects so that I track my time spent.

Sitemap

Now that I had a better idea of what my users needed I created a sitemap with intent efficiency, taking the case that students can track their time by having an app that helps them in a clear and organized way.

I wanted the users to get easy access to their timer, tasks, stats, forum, and settings without feeling overwhelmed.

Design

User flows

My user flows helped me understand how I wanted each screen to be laid out in order for users to complete their tasks. I chose three routes that were essential to iFocus and these were to identify a new task, start a timer, and community post.

Sketching

Sketching helped me bring those user flows to life. I realized quickly that I did not want many illustrations on screen, as well as a compact navigation menu. I wanted the experience to be simple and not overwhelming to the eye. After a few sketches I was content with the direction I was heading.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

I began working in Figma and brought those sketches into low fidelity wireframes. By doing so it helped clarify how I wanted functions spaced out on screen as well as giving me as much better insight on how I could simplify and prioritize functions.

Style guide

Taking into account all the information collected based on the users. I chose colors that are a bit calm but cheerful, to give a touch of joy and minimalism in the final design.

High Fidelity Wireframes

The main goal in the design process was to make a simplistic experience for users to follow and enjoy the process of staying focused. I made a few changes along the way allowing me to make better use of space and re construct certain aspects of the app.

Usability Test

Usability test

I was ready to test the app and conduct usability testing. I wanted to evaluate not only how users would complete tasks,  but the design as well as how they would navigate throughout the app. I put together the app prototype and gathered users that had previously taken the survey to not only test reaction to the design but give feedback on the ease of use for the app. All participants were students and they were content when interacting with the app prototype.

Testing Results

Overall, users did not find any big problematic issues or challenges during these tests.

Issue #1: One of the findings was; possible instructions for new users.

Summary: There wasn’t a screen to invite new users to learn about the app.

Recommendations: A popup offering instructions if the user wants them.

Prototype

After gathering all participants’ feedback I was then able to redesign to arrive at my final UI design.

Reflection

This project helped me on so many levels, from learning about students' life struggles to creating a solution to these problems. I enjoy creating solutions to these issues, this opens a new way that I see things now, I would love to implemented more features, such as, 'add a friend' with this feature the students will be able to study together in the same time lapse. I'm satisfied with the results of the final design, I will keep learning and helping the users to have enjoyable and memorable experiences.
